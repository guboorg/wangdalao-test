Vacuum Sintering Furnace Types by Hot Zone Design

Choose the hot zone and heating element that match your sintering temperature, atmosphere purity, workpiece size, contamination limits and budget.

01

Vacuum Stainless Steel Chamber Sintering Furnace

Suitable for lower-temperature processes requiring a robust metallic chamber, clean operation and economical maintenance.

02

Vacuum Ceramic Fiber Chamber Sintering Furnace

Designed for efficient insulation, faster heating response and flexible laboratory or batch-production workflows.

03

Vacuum Molybdenum Strip Furnace

Recommended for high-temperature, clean-metal hot zones where molybdenum heating offers stability and uniformity.

04

Vacuum Graphite Carbon Tube Furnace

Uses graphite/carbon tube heating for high temperatures, strong thermal efficiency and demanding powder metallurgy applications.

05

Vacuum Graphite Furnace

A versatile high-temperature option for cemented carbide, ceramics and advanced material sintering under vacuum or protective gas.

06

Vacuum Tungsten Wire Furnace

Built for extremely high-temperature, high-purity processes where tungsten heating elements are required.

Buying guide

What to Confirm Before Requesting a Quote

Providing clear process data helps our engineers recommend a practical furnace instead of overselling capacity you do not need.

  1. Material name, shape, size, batch weight and loading method.
  2. Maximum temperature, working temperature and temperature uniformity requirement.
  3. Required vacuum level, gas type, pressure control and cooling method.
  4. Working zone dimensions, cycle time and annual production capacity.
  5. Local voltage, plant utilities, installation space and automation expectations.
